## Summary

This Kumu ecosystem map from Digital Life Collective (DigLife) visualizes the landscape of decentralized technology — plotting hundreds of projects, protocols, and organizations across multiple domains: blockchain platforms, P2P networking, self-sovereign identity, decentralized storage, privacy tech, governance, and Web3 tooling.

Kumu is a relationship mapping tool — unlike a static diagram, the map is interactive and maintains entity relationships, tags, and descriptions. DigLife uses it to track the full ecosystem of decentralized alternatives to centralized platforms: IPFS and Filecoin for storage, Ethereum and Polkadot for programmable blockchains, ActivityPub and Matrix for decentralized communication, Ceramic and Solid for data sovereignty.

The value is orientation: the decentralization space is sprawling and terminology-heavy, with significant overlap and duplication across projects. A structured map that groups projects by function (storage, identity, computation, communication) and shows which ones interoperate helps navigate the ecosystem. It's particularly useful for distinguishing which Web3 projects are genuinely technically interesting from those that are primarily speculative.

## Key points

- Interactive Kumu relationship map covering 100s of decentralized tech projects and protocols
- Organized by function: P2P storage, blockchain, identity, privacy, communication, governance
- Includes IPFS, Ethereum, ActivityPub, Matrix, Solid, Filecoin, and many others
- From Digital Life Collective — focused on genuinely decentralized alternatives, not just crypto
- Useful orientation resource for navigating the sprawling Web3 and decentralization ecosystem
