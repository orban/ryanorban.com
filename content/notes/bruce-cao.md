## Summary

[Bruce Cao](/notes/bruce-cao/) is a designer whose portfolio centers on AI UX and interaction design. His stated focus is "crafting experiences with rhythm" — a directional brief that shows up across his projects in different ways.

Notable work includes a study on designing LLM interactions that encourage rabbit-holing (deep exploration rather than quick answers), a project improving icebreakers in Google Meet, a tool for converting loose thoughts into shareable writing, and a visual creativity concept aimed at snackable moments. Several projects are positioned as shipping in 2025/2026.

The LLM rabbit-holing project is the most distinctive — it addresses a real failure mode in AI assistants, where the interaction style tends toward completion and closure rather than curiosity and exploration. Designing interfaces that encourage depth over efficiency is an interesting counterpoint to most AI product work.

## Key points

- Designer focused on AI UX: how AI interactions feel and what behaviors they encourage.
- LLM design study on encouraging "rabbit-holing" — depth-first exploration vs. quick answers.
- Google Meet icebreaker redesign — social/human interaction layer on video meetings.
- Loose-thoughts-to-writing tool — turning informal ideas into shareable content.
- Portfolio across shipped and conceptual projects in AI interaction design.
