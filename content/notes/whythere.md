## Summary

[WhyThere](/notes/whythere/) is a web tool for comparing cities as relocation candidates using quantitative data rather than subjective impressions. The interface shows high-density visualizations side-by-side: weather patterns, cost of living, sunlight hours, proximity to nature, and other lifestyle factors. The design philosophy is explicitly data-over-feelings — factual comparison for a decision that often gets made on vibes.

The editorial content layer (beyond the comparison tool itself) covers surprising city comparisons, affordability analyses, climate doppelgängers, and tech hub growth patterns. This contextual content helps users understand what patterns mean, not just what numbers say.

For knowledge workers with location flexibility, tools like [WhyThere](/notes/whythere/) address a real decision quality problem: relocation is one of the highest-impact personal choices, yet people typically decide on inadequate data. The high-density visualization approach is important — you need to see multiple variables simultaneously, not read one-at-a-time statistics.

## Key points

- Side-by-side city comparison using quantitative data: weather, cost, sunlight, nature access.
- High-density visualizations designed to show multiple variables simultaneously.
- Editorial content supplements raw comparisons with context and patterns.
- Data-driven approach to relocation — counters subjective or anecdote-driven decision-making.
- Targets remote workers and knowledge workers with genuine location flexibility.
