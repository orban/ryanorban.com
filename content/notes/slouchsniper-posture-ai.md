## Summary

SlouchSniper is a macOS application that uses on-device computer vision to monitor sitting posture via webcam and provides behavioral feedback: when it detects slouching, it dims your screen. When you correct your posture, brightness restores. The entire inference runs locally — no data leaves the device. The feedback loop is designed to be gentle enough to avoid breaking concentration but noticeable enough to trigger correction.

The behavioral design is notable: screen dimming is a choice specifically because it's tied to the ongoing activity (working at the computer) without being intrusive like a notification or alarm. The goal is habitualization — training subconscious awareness of posture over time rather than requiring active attention. The founder reports it actually changed his posture habits.

The application runs with low battery impact and targets knowledge workers and students spending extended hours at computers. Priced at $38 one-time. This is a category of quantified self tools that use on-device AI to create behavioral feedback loops around body metrics without cloud dependency.

## Key points

- Real-time posture detection via webcam using on-device computer vision — no cloud required.
- Screen dimming as behavioral nudge: tied to ongoing activity, gentle but noticeable.
- Designed for habituation: subconscious posture improvement over time.
- Battery-efficient, low-friction — not intrusive like notifications.
- $38 one-time purchase, no subscription.
- Quantified self category: AI-powered behavioral feedback for body metrics.
