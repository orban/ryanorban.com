## Summary

This is a compact reference guide by Jesper Ordrup covering 21 vocal techniques across five categories: registers, styles, effects, embellishments, and dynamics. It's a static reference page — the kind of thing you bookmark and return to rather than read once.

Vocal technique is one of those domains where knowing the vocabulary matters. Terms like head voice, chest voice, falsetto, belting, melisma, vibrato, growl, and dynamics are used inconsistently across teaching traditions, and having a compact single-page reference that defines them precisely is useful. This kind of reference is especially valuable when learning from online resources, where instructors use different terminology for the same technique.

The 21-technique scope is well-chosen — broad enough to cover most of what appears in contemporary vocal coaching, focused enough to stay digestible. This sits alongside specialized references like Singing Success, Complete Vocal Technique, or various Seth Riggs materials.
