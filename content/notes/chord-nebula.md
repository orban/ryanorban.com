## Summary

[Chord Nebula](/notes/chord-nebula/) is an educational MIDI game for learning chord progressions on piano or keyboard. It requires a connected MIDI keyboard to play — you're not clicking buttons, you're actually playing the chords on real keys. The game presents progressions in a chosen key and difficulty level, you play along, and it scores your accuracy with immediate feedback. Optional displays can show either the individual notes in each chord or the harmonic function (tonic, dominant, subdominant, etc.) to connect sound to theory.

The progression from beginner to advanced is structured across five difficulty levels and covers both major scale and minor scale tonalities across all 14 keys (7 major + 7 minor). The lives-and-scoring mechanic is borrowed from game design — it creates stakes that pure practice software lacks, which helps maintain focus during repetitive chord drilling.

Ear training and chord recognition are genuinely hard to develop without regular, feedback-rich practice. Apps like [Chord Nebula](/notes/chord-nebula/) make the practice loop tighter: you play something, you immediately know if it was right or wrong, and the system tracks your improvement. This is more effective than passive listening or flashcard-style apps. Related to music theory learning tools and the broader category of gamified music education.

## Key points

- Requires a real MIDI keyboard — not a simulation, actual playing practice.
- Five difficulty levels, 14 keys (major and minor), chord progression training.
- Toggle between note display and harmonic function view (tonic/dominant/subdominant).
- Scoring + lives system adds game stakes to chord practice.
- Useful for bridging the gap between knowing theory and hearing/playing it fluently.
