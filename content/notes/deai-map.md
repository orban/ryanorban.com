## Summary

[DeAI Map](/notes/deai-map/) is a crowdsourced visual map of the Decentralized AI ecosystem, hosted by Topology VC. It maps projects working on distributed compute, decentralized training, on-chain AI inference, privacy-preserving ML, and related infrastructure — essentially the intersection of AI and Web3 / distributed systems.

The Decentralized AI space includes several distinct threads: federated learning (train without centralizing data), distributed inference networks (Bittensor, Akash), on-chain model ownership and provenance, homomorphic encryption for private inference, and verifiable compute using zero-knowledge proofs. These threads are sometimes grouped together but have different technical approaches and maturity levels.

From a research perspective, the map is useful as a landscape reference — understanding which projects exist, how they cluster, and what problems they address. Whether Decentralized AI becomes a significant infrastructure layer or remains a niche remains an open question; the map's crowdsourced nature means it reflects community interest rather than rigorous curation.

## Key points

- Crowdsourced landscape map of the Decentralized AI ecosystem, maintained by Topology VC.
- Covers: distributed compute, decentralized training, on-chain inference, privacy-preserving ML.
- Key projects in the space: Bittensor, Akash, Gensyn, Ritual.
- Multiple distinct technical threads: federated learning, distributed inference, verifiable compute.
- Useful as a reference for understanding the DeAI landscape rather than a curated recommendation list.
- Adjacent to the GPU calculator (inference.ai) for understanding AI compute options.
