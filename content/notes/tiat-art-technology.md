## Summary

TIAT (The Intersection of Art & Technology) is a gallery and creative space at 151 Powell Street in San Francisco, dedicated to work that uses technology as artistic medium. It functions simultaneously as a public gallery, event venue, and membership coworking/studio space for creative technologists.

The programming structure is telling: monthly Salons feature practitioner presentations and demos (TIAT has run 15+), public exhibitions translate emerging tech into cultural insights, and workshops let practitioners teach techniques directly. This is not a passive gallery — it's organized around the idea that making and community go together. Members get coworking and studio access Monday-Saturday, turning it into a working space, not just an event destination.

The kinds of work featured — visual synthesis, computational poetry, haptic installations, AI-generated art, creative coding — map to the broader creative technologist community that intersects with tools like Processing, p5.js, and TouchDesigner. San Francisco is a natural home for this; TIAT offers the physical infrastructure that maker communities need but often lack.

## Key points

- Gallery + coworking hybrid: public exhibitions plus member studio access.
- Monthly Salons: 15+ events featuring creative technologist presentations.
- SF location (151 Powell St): gallery hours Thu-Fri 12-6pm, Sat 12-5pm.
- Focus areas: creative coding, generative art, haptic design, AI art, interactive installation.
- Community-building mission: a place for creative technologists to experiment, exhibit, and connect.
- Shop and commission options for featured artists.
