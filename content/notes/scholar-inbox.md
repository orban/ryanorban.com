## Summary

[Scholar Inbox](/notes/scholar-inbox/) is a paper recommendation service that delivers daily email digests of new academic papers matched to your research interests. The personalization is based on papers you've engaged with — liked, saved, or from your publication record if you link your Google Scholar profile. The result is a daily feed of papers in your areas of interest from arXiv and related preprint repositories, prioritized by relevance.

The problem it solves is real: keeping up with the research literature is genuinely hard. arXiv publishes hundreds of papers daily across ML/AI alone; Google Scholar alerts are blunt instruments (keyword-based, many false positives). [Scholar Inbox](/notes/scholar-inbox/) aims to be a smarter filter — learning what you actually care about rather than just matching keywords. The email format is intentional: papers come to your inbox rather than requiring you to check another app.

Comparable tools include Semantic Scholar's recommendations, ResearchRabbit, and Elicit. Scholar Inbox's angle is the daily digest email rather than a web interface you have to visit. This is a meaningful distinction for researchers who live in email rather than browser tabs. Most competitive with Semantic Scholar's recommendation feature, which has similar personalization but a different interaction model.

## Key points

- Daily email digest of relevant papers — personalized rather than keyword-based.
- Learns from your reading history and linked Google Scholar profile.
- Sources papers from arXiv and similar preprint repositories.
- Competes with Semantic Scholar alerts, ResearchRabbit, and Elicit.
- Email-first format — no additional app to check.
- Useful for ML/AI researchers overwhelmed by arXiv publication volume.
