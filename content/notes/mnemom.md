## Summary

[Mnemom](/notes/mnemom/) is an AI agent explainability tool with the tagline \"Prove What Your AI Agents Are Thinking.\" The focus is on making agent reasoning transparent and auditable — not just observing what agents do, but understanding why they made the decisions they did.

This fills a specific gap in the AI agent observability space. Tools like [Agent Super Spy](/notes/agent-super-spy/), tapes, and [Context Lens](/notes/context-lens/) show what agents execute: API calls, tool invocations, context composition. Mnemom appears to focus on the reasoning layer — explaining or validating the thought process behind agent decisions, not just the actions themselves. This is closer to interpretability than observability.

As agents take on more consequential autonomous actions, the need to explain and validate their reasoning becomes a compliance and trust issue, not just a debugging tool.

## Key points

- Agent reasoning explainability: \"Prove What Your AI Agents Are Thinking.\"
- Distinct from execution observability (tapes, [Agent Super Spy](/notes/agent-super-spy/)) — focuses on decision reasoning.
- Addresses the interpretability gap in AI agent development and deployment.
- Available at mnemom.ai.
