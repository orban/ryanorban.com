## Summary

Handy is a free, open-source speech-to-text application that works system-wide: press a keyboard shortcut, speak, release, and the transcribed text pastes into whatever field is active. All processing happens locally — no audio sent to cloud services, no account required.

The push-to-talk model is deliberately simple. There's no mode management, no dictation wake words, no continuous listening. Press to record, release to transcribe and paste. Keyboard shortcuts are configurable; toggle mode is also available.

In the speech-to-text landscape, this sits closest to Whisper-powered local tools. Compared to Monologue (paid, with cloud features) and Spokenly (freemium with cloud AI), Handy is the fully-local, zero-cost option. The cross-platform support covers macOS, Windows, and Linux.

## Key points

- Push-to-talk: hold shortcut to record, release to transcribe and paste into active field.
- Fully local processing — no audio uploaded, no cloud dependency.
- Free and open-source — available on all platforms (macOS, Windows, Linux).
- Configurable keyboard shortcuts; toggle mode available.
- No accounts, no subscriptions, no telemetry.
- Compare to Monologue, Spokenly — more minimal and fully local.
