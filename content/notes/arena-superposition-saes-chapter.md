## Summary

ARENA (Alignment Research Engineer Accelerator) is a structured curriculum for learning mechanistic interpretability and AI safety research skills. Chapter 1.3.1 covers Toy Models of Superposition and Sparse Autoencoders — the theoretical foundation and practical implementation of SAE-based interpretability research.

Toy Models of Superposition refers to a specific paper by Anthropic researchers that demonstrated how neural networks store more features than they have neurons via superposition: representing multiple independent features in overlapping directions, relying on sparsity to reduce interference. This chapter builds from the toy model to understanding why SAEs are the right tool: they're trained to reverse the compression, recovering the sparse features from dense activations.

The ARENA curriculum is interactive — Streamlit-based with embedded exercises — and is widely used in the mechanistic interpretability community for onboarding. It sits alongside Neel Nanda's transformer circuits tutorials and TransformerLens as core resources for learning this field. For anyone interested in AI interpretability research, Chapter 1.3 (and its 1.3.1 extension) is the standard deep dive into SAEs.
