## Summary

CommonCog is a discussion community centered on serious business analysis — described as "sensemaking for business nerds." The forum is attached to Cedric Chin's writing practice, which focuses on how expertise actually develops in business (drawing heavily on cognitive science research like Naturalistic Decision Making) and rigorous case study analysis of great operators.

The content clusters around a few themes: case studies of historical operators (Charlie Munger, Tom Murphy, Jim Kilts), analysis of business frameworks (Jobs to be Done, Amazon's PR/FAQ, Innovator's Dilemma), and the meta-question of how to actually get good at business rather than just learning about it. Cedric's writing is unusual in that it's genuinely trying to extract tacit knowledge from case studies rather than surface-level lessons.

CommonCog occupies a specific niche in the business learning ecosystem: it's more rigorous than typical business content (less narrative, more analytical), less academic than strategy journals, and more operationally focused than pure theory. The Naturalistic Decision Making influence is evident — the interest is in how experts actually make decisions under uncertainty, not idealized models. It sits alongside resources like Farnam Street, Stratechery, and Acquired in the serious-business-learning space but with more focus on developing practitioner SKILL than just information consumption.

## Key points

- Run by Cedric Chin — known for rigorous writing on business expertise and Naturalistic Decision Making.
- Case studies: Charlie Munger, Tom Murphy, Jim Kilts — deep dives into actual operator decisions.
- Framework focus: Jobs to be Done, Amazon PR/FAQ, Innovator's Dilemma analysis.
- Meta-theme: how to develop genuine business expertise, not just accumulate information.
- Sits alongside Farnam Street, Acquired, Stratechery in quality-tier business learning.
- Community format: structured discussion, not casual chatter.
