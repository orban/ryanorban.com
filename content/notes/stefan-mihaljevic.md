## Summary

[Stefan Mihaljevic](/notes/stefan-mihaljevic/) is a depth psychologist and psychotherapist focused on high-achieving individuals who've reached external markers of success but feel unfulfilled. His practice centers on shadow work — examining what we avoid, the unconscious patterns that drive behavior — rather than symptom management or surface-level coping. His framing: "The only way out is in."

The modalities he uses include integrative psychotherapy, depth psychology, dreamwork, and shadow integration. The dreamwork in particular connects to the Jungian tradition, where dreams are treated as communications from parts of the psyche that don't surface in ordinary conscious reflection.

His explicit not for list is useful framing: not for people seeking validation without challenge, quick fixes, or who attribute suffering entirely to external circumstances. This positions the work as demanding, introspective, and appropriate for people willing to sit with discomfort rather than move past it.

## Key points

- Focuses on shadow integration — examining avoided aspects of personality rather than cultivating positive ones alone.
- Dreamwork as a primary modality alongside talk therapy — Jungian in orientation.
- Target client: externally successful but internally unfulfilled — the archetypal "achieved everything, still not happy" situation.
- Explicitly not for people seeking quick fixes or external validation — requires genuine willingness for uncomfortable self-examination.
- M.Sc. in Psychology, 8 years practice, based in Serbia, global clients via Google Meet.
