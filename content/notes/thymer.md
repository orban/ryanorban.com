## Summary

[Thymer](/notes/thymer/) is a browser-based planning tool that combines outlining, note-taking, and task management. The positioning is interesting: it explicitly rejects the shopping list model of task managers (rigid, checklist-oriented) in favor of treating planning as a creative process. The editor borrows conventions from developer tools — command palette, split panels, keyboard shortcuts, focus mode — and applies them to thought organization.

The core feature set covers what you'd want for serious planning work: flexible outlining with combined prose and structure, real-time collaboration with live cursors, customizable databases and properties, end-to-end encryption even during collaboration, and offline support with automatic sync. The emphasis on encryption and self-hosting options is a deliberate privacy stance — the user owns their data regardless of whether they use the hosted or self-hosted version.

Thymer competes directly with Notion (rich but heavy), Roam Research (graph-based, clunky interface), Obsidian (local-first, plugin-heavy), and Logseq (outliner + graph). Its differentiator is the speed-of-thought emphasis combined with structure — it wants to be fast like a scratchpad but organized like a database. The end-to-end encryption in the collaborative tier is a real differentiator since most cloud collaboration tools don't offer that. Niche but well-positioned for privacy-sensitive teams.

## Key points

- Outlining + prose + task management in one editor — resists over-structuring.
- Developer-style UX: command palette, keyboard shortcuts, split panels, focus mode.
- Real-time collaboration with end-to-end encryption — unusual for collaborative tools.
- Self-hosting option: data ownership regardless of service provider.
- Offline support with automatic sync.
- Positions between Notion (heavy) and plain text files (unstructured).
