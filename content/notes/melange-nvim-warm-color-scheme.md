## Summary

Melange is a Neovim color scheme built around warm amber, brown, and ochre tones — an alternative to the cool blue/purple schemes that dominate the Neovim ecosystem. It supports both dark and light variants with consistent color logic across both. The name refers to a blending/mixing of warm tones.

The scheme is written in Lua using Lush.nvim (a Neovim color scheme framework), with exports for terminal emulators (Alacritty, Kitty, WezTerm) and other tools. Melange is often compared to Rosé Pine and Gruvbox — all three occupy the warm-tone space, but Melange's amber tones are more consistent and the contrast ratios are carefully calibrated.

For developers who spend long hours in the terminal, warm color schemes reduce eye strain under certain lighting conditions. The trend toward warm/dark schemes (Melange, Kanagawa, Everforest) reflects broader aesthetic preferences away from high-contrast cool-blue defaults.

## Key points

- Warm amber/brown palette with dark and light variants.
- Written in Lua with Lush.nvim; exports for Alacritty, Kitty, WezTerm.
- In the same space as Gruvbox and Rosé Pine but with more consistent amber tones.
- Maintained by savq on GitHub.
