## Summary

Resilient Cyber maintains a curated collection of AI security interviews, publications, and frameworks. The collection aggregates the practitioner-facing side of AI security: how to think about LLM threat models, what attack surfaces are specific to AI systems, and how existing security frameworks need to extend to cover AI.

The AI security landscape in 2025 is fragmented — OWASP LLM Top 10, MITRE ATLAS, NIST AI RMF, and various vendor guidance documents all exist but aren't well-integrated. A curated collection like this serves as a synthesis layer for practitioners trying to figure out what's actually relevant. Resilient Cyber (Chris Hughes' publication) has been tracking the intersection of cybersecurity and AI seriously, covering topics like prompt injection, model poisoning, supply chain attacks on AI systems, and AI red teaming.

The collection format (interviews + publications + frameworks) reflects how AI security knowledge is currently distributed: partly in academic papers, partly in practitioner blog posts, and partly in vendor frameworks that are sometimes self-serving. Having a curated view helps avoid both the academic-only and vendor-only biases.

## Key points

- Aggregates AI security resources: interviews with practitioners, academic publications, and governance frameworks.
- Covers OWASP LLM Top 10, MITRE ATLAS, and other AI-specific security standards.
- Useful for practitioners bridging traditional cybersecurity expertise into AI systems contexts.
- Topics span: prompt injection, model poisoning, supply chain attacks, AI red teaming.
- From Resilient Cyber (Chris Hughes) — practitioner-focused, not purely academic.
