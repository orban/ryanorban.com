## Summary

This OPSEC guide (by Zycher) is a community-produced reference for operational security practices — the discipline of protecting sensitive information from adversaries by managing what information you reveal and when. OPSEC originated in military contexts but applies directly to activists, journalists, security researchers, and privacy-conscious individuals.

Operational security starts with threat modeling: who are your adversaries, what do they want, and what capabilities do they have? The answer determines which controls are worth the friction. For most individuals the threat is surveillance capitalism and data brokers; for higher-risk individuals (activists, journalists in authoritarian countries) the threat model is more serious and the required controls more rigorous.

Core OPSEC practices typically include: reducing personal information online (data broker opt-outs, social media hygiene), using end-to-end encrypted communications (Signal, encrypted email), separating identities across contexts, managing metadata (which carries as much information as content), and physical security awareness. The guide likely provides a structured walkthrough of these areas for non-expert readers.

## Key points

- OPSEC = protecting sensitive information through behavioral discipline, not just tools.
- Starts with threat modeling: calibrate controls to actual adversary capabilities.
- Key areas: information reduction, encrypted communications, identity separation, metadata hygiene.
- Community-produced reference, likely oriented toward privacy-conscious individuals.
