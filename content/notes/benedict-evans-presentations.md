## Summary

Benedict Evans is a tech industry analyst known for producing two major presentations per year that distill macro trends in the technology industry into accessible strategic frameworks. The presentations are free PDFs, typically several hundred slides, covering shifts in platforms, business models, consumer behavior, and emerging technologies. His newsletter reaches ~200,000 subscribers.

His 2025 edition is titled AI eats the world — a play on Marc Andreessen's software is eating the world framing, updated for the current moment. Evans's previous presentations have covered mobile's dominance, the decline of retail, streaming's disruption of media, and the concentration of tech platforms. The AI eats everything thesis continues this pattern: each wave of technology restructures industries in ways that feel gradual from the inside but look sudden in retrospect.

What makes Evans's work useful is the curation. He's processing a large volume of industry data (public company filings, regulatory reports, market research) and presenting only the signals that seem most load-bearing for understanding the next 2-5 years. He's particularly strong on second-order effects — not just AI changes search but what that means for advertising markets, publishing, enterprise software, and the labor market for knowledge workers.

## Key points

- Free annual PDF presentations — typically 200-400 slides, downloadable without subscription.
- 2025 edition: "AI eats the world" — AI's structural effects on tech and adjacent industries.
- Benedict Evans previously at Andreessen Horowitz, now independent.
- ~200,000 newsletter subscribers — widely read across VC, enterprise, and tech policy circles.
- Strong on structural analysis: platforms, market concentration, regulatory implications.
- Good starting point for annual orientation on where the technology industry is heading.
