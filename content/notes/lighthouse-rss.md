## Summary

Lighthouse is an RSS feed reader built around the inbox-zero philosophy — designed to make the act of clearing your feed queue feel satisfying rather than overwhelming. Its newsletter-to-RSS tool is particularly useful: it provides a unique email address that converts any email newsletter subscription into an RSS feed, letting you read newsletters in your RSS reader alongside your regular feeds.

The problem this solves: newsletters and RSS feeds are separate information streams that require separate management habits. Email newsletters go to your inbox (where they compete with actionable email), while RSS feeds live in a reader. By converting newsletters to RSS, Lighthouse unifies information consumption into one place with one workflow.

The inbox zero metaphor applied to RSS is a useful framing: rather than feeds being an endless scroll, they have a state (read/unread) and the goal is to clear them intentionally. This sits alongside tools like Readwise Reader, Matter, and Feedly in the manage your reading workflow space.

## Key points

- Converts email newsletters to RSS feeds — unique email address per subscription.
- Unifies newsletters and RSS feeds into a single inbox-style reading interface.
- Inbox zero UX applied to feeds — intentional clearing rather than endless scroll.
- Competes with Readwise Reader, Matter, Feedly in the reading workflow space.
- Addresses information overload by consolidating information streams.
