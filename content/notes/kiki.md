## Summary

[Kiki](/notes/kiki/) is a macOS app that enforces single-tasking by showing only one thing at a time. It's pitched as an "accountability monster for people who are easily distracted" — the design choice of one-thing-at-a-time removes the constant temptation to switch contexts that most productivity apps leave intact.

The concept is simple to the point of being almost obvious, which is probably its strength. Most productivity systems accumulate enough surface area that managing the system becomes a task itself. [Kiki](/notes/kiki/) takes the opposite approach: the only job is to define the one current thing and then do it.

This category of tool has grown alongside AI-assisted development, where the human's role is increasingly about direction and judgment rather than implementation — which puts a premium on sustained focus and clear priorities over parallelism.

## Key points

- Forces single-tasking by design — one thing visible at a time, no multitasking surface.
- Accountability monster framing: the app's job is to hold you to staying on one task.
- macOS app, for developers and knowledge workers prone to context-switching.
- Minimal by design — feature restraint is the point.
