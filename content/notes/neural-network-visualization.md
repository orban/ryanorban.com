## Summary

This interactive visualization from visualrambling.space builds intuition for how neural networks work through direct manipulation rather than passive diagrams. The design goal is the same one that makes tools like 3Blue1Brown's video series valuable: concrete visual representation of abstract mathematical operations, so you can develop a mental model of what the math is doing.

Feedforward neural networks have a well-understood structure — layers of nodes connected by weighted edges, activations flowing forward, gradients flowing back during backpropagation — but the leap from the math to intuition is not trivial for most people. Visualizations that let you adjust weights and watch activations change, or that show gradient flow during training, can make this click in ways that equations alone don't.

This sits in a category of tools that focus on machine learning education through interaction: TensorFlow Playground, Neural Network Playground, Andrej Karpathy's micrograd. The value is building the kind of intuition that makes the higher-level abstractions (transformers, attention, residual connections) feel less magical.

## Key points

- Interactive visualization of neural network mechanics — weights, activations, and backpropagation through direct manipulation.
- Builds intuition rather than just presenting formulas.
- Same educational niche as TensorFlow Playground and 3Blue1Brown series on neural networks.
- Good reference for anyone learning machine learning fundamentals or teaching them.
