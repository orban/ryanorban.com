## Summary

This YouTube documentary is part of a series on the Nabataeans — the ancient Arab civilization that built Petra in what is now southern Jordan. Episode 15 covers their final days: the decline of Nabataean independence and the incorporation of their territory into the Roman Empire as the province of Arabia Petraea in 106 CE under Emperor Trajan.

The Nabataeans were remarkable for their time: a nomadic Arab people who built a sophisticated trade empire controlling the incense routes from Arabia and India to the Mediterranean. Petra — carved into rose-red sandstone cliffs — was their capital, a city of temples, tombs, and a sophisticated water management system that allowed a city to survive in an arid desert. At their peak, the Nabataeans controlled trade routes worth enormous fortunes in frankincense, myrrh, spices, and silk.

Their decline is a study in geopolitical shift: the rise of sea routes from Egypt to India bypassed the overland Nabataean routes, reducing their commercial power. When Rome annexed their kingdom, it was largely a peaceful transition — the Nabataeans had already lost much of their strategic importance. Petra was eventually abandoned and forgotten by the Western world until Johann Ludwig Burckhardt rediscovered it in 1812.
