## Summary

[Animated Knots](/notes/animated-knots/) (by Grog) is the web's primary reference for learning to tie knots, with step-by-step animated demonstrations that show ropes tying themselves while highlighting key steps. The animations make it significantly more learnable than static diagrams.

The content is organized two ways: by activity (climbing, sailing, fishing, scouting, search & rescue, household, arborist, horse & farm, neckties, decorative, surgical) and by type (bends, hitches, loops, stoppers, splices, etc.). The surgical knots section is notable — knot knowledge crosses domains from marine to medical.

This is the kind of reference site that earns a permanent bookmark. Knot-tying is a physical skill that benefits enormously from animated instruction. The iOS and Android apps extend the reference offline, which is practical for field use. Technical resources on rope materials, properties, and safety round out the reference.

## Key points

- Animated step-by-step instructions for knot-tying — animated ropes, highlighted key steps.
- Organized by activity (climbing, sailing, fishing, surgical, scouting) and by knot type.
- Covers 12+ activity domains including surgical knots.
- iOS and Android apps for offline field use.
- Includes technical resources on rope properties and safety.
