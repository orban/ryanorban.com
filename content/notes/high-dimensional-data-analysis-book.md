## Summary

High-Dimensional Data Analysis by John Wright and Yi Ma is a graduate textbook covering the mathematical foundations for analyzing and learning from high-dimensional data. The distinguishing framing is unification: sparse models, low-rank models, and deep networks are treated as instances of a common mathematical framework rather than separate topics requiring separate machinery.

The content spans sparse representation (compressed sensing, dictionary learning), low-rank models (PCA, robust PCA, matrix completion), and deep networks — covering both convex and nonconvex approaches to the underlying optimization problems. Application domains include scientific imaging, communications, face recognition, 3D vision, and neural networks.

This is a textbook for researchers and graduate students who want to understand the mathematical structure underlying modern machine learning, not a practitioner guide for applying existing tools. Adoption at UC Berkeley, Columbia, UIUC, and Tsinghua signals academic credibility. A preproduction version is freely available online.

## Key points

- Unifies sparse models, low-rank models, and deep networks under one mathematical framework.
- Both convex and nonconvex optimization approaches covered — not just the easy cases.
