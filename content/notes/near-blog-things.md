## Summary

A personal product recommendation page from the blogger "near" — a curated list of items they've bought and found genuinely useful, organized by category: health and wellness, sleep, personal care, home organization, lighting, tech and gadgets, kitchen, cable management, and miscellaneous. The author's frame is explicitly problem-solving: find the thing that addresses the friction, buy it, move on.

The list includes items like CO2 monitors, Zbiotics (a hangover-reducing probiotic), weighted blankets, bidet attachments, smart bulbs, and various organizational hardware. The author notes that "almost everything is really cheap" — this isn't a luxury product list, it's a pragmatic one.

Pages like this are useful reference points in the genre of "things smart people actually buy" — a counterpart to the curated recommendation lists from Patrick OShaughnessy, Dan Luu, and others who share gear lists. The signal value is that actual use and honest evaluation sits behind each item rather than affiliate-motivated selection.
