## Summary

The Startup CTO's Handbook is a free book by Zach Goldberg published as an open markdown file on GitHub. It covers the full scope of the startup CTO role: technical leadership, engineering management, hiring, team structure, architecture decisions, and the organizational dynamics that make or break engineering orgs at early-stage companies.

The book's focus on startups specifically is useful because the CTO role at a startup is fundamentally different from a VP Eng at a large company. At a startup, the CTO is often still coding, making individual technical decisions, building the team from scratch, and operating with incomplete information and rapidly changing requirements. The handbook covers this tension directly — when to be hands-on vs. hands-off, how to build process without bureaucracy, when to hire generalists vs. specialists.

Key topics include: hiring and interviewing, managing engineers (including performance issues), technical debt and its relationship to speed, system design decisions at scale, communication with non-technical founders and board members, and building engineering culture. The GitHub format means it's community-editable and stays current. Similar in spirit to The Manager's Path (Camille Fournier) for individual contributor to manager transitions, but focused specifically on the CTO/technical co-founder trajectory at startups.

## Key points

- Free, open-source: single Markdown file on GitHub, community contributions welcome.
- Covers leadership, management, and technical topics specific to startup engineering leaders.
- Topics: hiring, managing engineers, technical debt, system design, communication with non-technical stakeholders.
- Distinguishes the startup CTO role from VP Eng at larger companies — hands-on, incomplete information, rapid change.
- Companion to The Manager's Path (Camille Fournier) for technical leadership education.
