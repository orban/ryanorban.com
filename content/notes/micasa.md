## Summary

[micasa](/notes/micasa/) is a terminal application for managing home maintenance — tracking tasks, projects, vendor information, quotes, and costs from a keyboard-driven interface. The niche is specific: it's for the kind of person who finds a dedicated terminal app more ergonomic than a spreadsheet or home management app, and who has enough ongoing maintenance to benefit from tracking it systematically.

The feature set covers the practical needs: maintenance schedules (when did you last change the furnace filter, when is it due), project tracking from initial idea through completion, vendor and contractor contact info, side-by-side quote comparison, and spending by category. Vim-style modal navigation throughout. Works offline, with optional local AI analysis — useful for things like "is this quote reasonable for this type of work."

The niche appeal is clear: this serves a specific type of developer who prefers living in the terminal and applies programmer habits (structured tracking, keyboard-driven workflow) to home ownership.

## Key points

- Terminal app for home maintenance — maintenance schedules, projects, vendors, quotes, and cost tracking.
- Vim-style modal keyboard navigation — no mouse required.
- Quote comparison: side-by-side viewing for informed decisions on contractors.
- Works offline with optional local AI analysis.
- Sample data available to try before committing your own home's data.
